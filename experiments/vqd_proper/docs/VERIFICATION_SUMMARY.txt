Finding 1: Original Results Inflated by Unfair Comparison
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  • Original used DIFFERENT centering for PCA vs VQD
  • PCA: global centering (sklearn default)
  • VQD: per-sequence centering  
  • This gave VQD unfair ~10-15% boost

Finding 2: After Fair Comparison, VQD Still Wins (But Modestly)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  • With aligned methods: VQD gains +2-5% over PCA
  • Advantage is smaller but consistent across seeds
  • VQD finds different subspaces (angles ~90°)

Finding 3: Centering Choice Matters A LOT
──────────────────────────────────────────
  • Global centering: baseline ~68%, lower overall
  • Per-sequence centering: baseline ~76%, higher overall
  • Per-sequence better preserves temporal structure

Finding 4: No Bugs, Label Leakage, or Unfair Advantages
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  ✓ Data loading correct
  ✓ No label leakage (shuffled → chance)
  ✓ Projection methods consistent
  ✓ VQD optimization works (ortho < 1e-12)

Finding 5: VQD Advantage is Real but Modest
────────────────────────────────────────────
  • Not the dramatic 16.7% initially observed
  • But a consistent 2-5% improvement
  • Reproducible across different splits
